Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does custom software development take?

Development timelines vary based on complexity. A simple MVP typically takes 3-4 months, while enterprise-grade applications range from 6-12 months. We provide detailed timelines during the discovery phase.

What is the cost of custom software development?

Costs depend on project scope, complexity, and technology stack. Simple applications start around $50,000, while complex enterprise solutions can range from $200,000-$500,000+. We offer flexible engagement models including fixed-price and dedicated team options.

Do you sign NDAs and protect intellectual property?

Absolutely. We sign NDAs before any project discussions and ensure full IP rights transfer to you upon project completion. All our developers sign confidentiality agreements.

Can you integrate with our existing systems?

Yes, we specialize in system integration using APIs, webhooks, and enterprise integration patterns. We've integrated with CRMs (Salesforce, HubSpot), ERPs (SAP, Oracle), payment gateways, and custom legacy systems.

Which cloud platform should we choose?

The choice depends on your specific needs, existing infrastructure, and budget. AWS is often chosen for its maturity, Azure for Microsoft ecosystem integration, and GCP for data analytics and ML capabilities. We provide platform assessments.

Do we need large amounts of data for AI solutions?

Not always. We use techniques like transfer learning, pre-trained models, and synthetic data generation for projects with limited datasets. We assess data requirements during the discovery phase.

What development methodology do you use?

We primarily use Agile/Scrum with 2-week sprints, daily standups, and regular sprint reviews. For larger projects, we may use scaled agile frameworks. We adapt our methodology to fit your organization's workflow.

What about ongoing maintenance and support?

We offer various support tiers including 24/7 monitoring, bug fixes, security patches, performance optimization, and feature enhancements. SLAs are defined based on your business criticality.
